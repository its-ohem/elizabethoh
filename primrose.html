"EVENING PRIMROSE"


"Evening Primrose" was written for an Intro to Screenwriting class and is one of my first full-length creative works I produced in college. In fact, it was this class that encouraged me to declare a Creative Writing major. Writing in and studying the screenplay format was incredibly engaging -- it was as though I had discovered an entirely new medium for storytelling. "Evening Primrose" is my take on a queer rom-com: when a woman's car breaks down, she must seek refuge in an unlikely place with a woman-run family out in the Mojave Desert. It was fun to write and push myself out of my comfort zone by exploring new and unfamiliar genres.

After several workshopping sessions and consultations, I believed that "Evening Primrose" had a shot in the University of Cincinnati's English Department's Writing Prize Competition 2021. I submitted it to the Screenplay Prize category for screenplays shorter than ten pages, and a few weeks later I was awarded Best Screenplay. To have been awarded for a story in an entirely new medium of writing was beyond thrilling, and still I am very proud of my achievement.
